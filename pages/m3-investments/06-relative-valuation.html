相对估值 — MultiplesRelative Valuation — Multiples

"市场对这家公司付了多少钱?" DCF 是绝对估值, 相对估值是看同类资产的市场定价做对照。两者互为补充: DCF 给"应该值多少", 倍数给"市场愿意付多少"。Damodaran 推荐并用。"What does the market pay for this firm?" Multiples reflect market pricing of comparables; complement DCF (should‑be vs market‑willing).

学习目标Objectives
  • 用 5 个直觉维度比较 老李 面馆和 老张 面馆, 类推到公司估值倍数
  • 掌握 P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, P/S, PEG 的适用场景与陷阱
  • 区分 trailing vs forward 倍数, 理解周期股 P/E 反转之谜
  • 从 Gordon 模型推出 justified P/E, 把倍数与基本面 ROE / g / r 对接
  • 用相对估值反推市场对该股的隐含增长率, 再判断合理性
  • 对比 招行 vs 工行 (P/B 与 ROE 关系) 和 茅台 vs 五粮液 (P/E premium 拆解)
  • Compare two noodle stalls on 5 intuitive metrics, then map to corporate multiples
  • Master P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, P/S, PEG — when each applies and where each breaks
  • Trailing vs forward; the cyclical P/E inversion puzzle

  • Reverse engineer market's implied growth and judge it

Layer 1 · 直觉Intuition · Two noodle stalls, five comparison angles · 两家面馆, 五个比较维度

上一章 老王 用 DCF 给 老李 面馆估了值。今天他又遇到一件事: 巷子另一头还有一家 老张 面馆, 也在转让。两家面馆 老王 都想买, 但只能买一家, 必须比较出哪家"更值"。问题是 — 该比什么?

In the last chapter Old Wang DCF'd Old Li's stall. Today there's another twist: down the alley is a second stall, Old Zhang's, also for sale. Wang would buy either, but can only afford one. He must compare. Compare what, exactly?

维度 1 — 每碗面卖多少钱 ÷ 每天卖几碗。 老王 第一个想到的就是售价。老李 面馆每碗 18 元, 一天卖 200 碗; 老张 面馆每碗 22 元, 一天卖 180 碗。每天营收: 老李 3,600 元, 老张 3,960 元。营收差不多, 但定价方式不同 — 老李 走量, 老张 走"附近最贵"路线。老王 问: "营收差不多, 怎么知道谁更值钱?"答: 营收只是输入, 还要看谁能更高效地把营收转成利润。这就是后面要说的 P/S (市值除以营收) 比率: 同样 1 元营收, 市场愿意为谁付更多钱?如果两家面馆都开价 100 万, 但 老李 一年营收 130 万, 老张 一年营收 144 万, 那 老张 的 P/S 更低 — 每元营收换来的报价更低, 在营收口径上 老张 看起来"更便宜"。

Angle 1 — price per bowl × bowls per day. Old Li: ¥18 × 200/day = ¥3,600/day. Old Zhang: ¥22 × 180/day = ¥3,960/day. Revenue is similar but pricing strategy differs — Li runs volume, Zhang charges the alley's max. "Same revenue, who's worth more?" — revenue is an input; the question is who turns revenue into profit efficiently. This is the P/S (price‑to‑sales) ratio: per ¥1 of revenue, how much is the market paying? If both asking prices are ¥1m but Li does ¥1.3m and Zhang ¥1.44m annually, Zhang's P/S is lower — by revenue Zhang looks "cheaper."

维度 2 — 每年赚多少钱。 老王 翻账本: 老李 营收 130 万, 净利 25 万 (净利率 19%); 老张 营收 144 万, 净利 18 万 (净利率 12.5%)。咦 — 老张 营收高, 但净利反而低。为什么?老张 房租贵 (位置好), 进货价高 (用更高档食材)。从盈利角度看, 老李 反而强。如果都开价 100 万, 老李 P/E (price ÷ earnings) = 100/25 = 4 倍; 老张 P/E = 100/18 ≈ 5.6 倍。同样的售价, 老李 一年回本得更快, P/E 越低越便宜。

Angle 2 — annual profit. Li: ¥1.3m revenue, ¥250k net (19% margin). Zhang: ¥1.44m revenue, ¥180k net (12.5% margin). Higher revenue, lower profit — Zhang pays for prime rent and premium ingredients. At ¥1m ask: Li P/E = 4, Zhang P/E ≈ 5.6. By earnings Li is cheaper — faster payback per yuan of price.

维度 3 — 店里有多少"固定资产"。 老李 店面 50 平米, 桌椅 20 套, 厨房设备齐全, 装修近 3 年, 估清算价 40 万。老张 店面 35 平米, 桌椅 15 套, 设备较旧, 清算价 25 万。100 万开价对比清算价: 老李 P/B (price ÷ book) = 100/40 = 2.5; 老张 P/B = 100/25 = 4。在资产口径上 老李 又便宜。但这不意味着 老李 一定更好 — 维度 3 反映"店倒了能卖多少破烂", 而面馆主要价值在未来现金流, 不在桌椅。所以 P/B 在面馆这种轻资产 + 看品牌的生意上参考价值有限; 但在"店主要价值就是有形资产"的行业 (银行、重工、地产) 就关键 — 后面会讲。

Angle 3 — physical assets. Li: 50 m², 20 tables, fitted kitchen, liquidation ¥400k. Zhang: 35 m², 15 tables, older gear, liquidation ¥250k. P/B at ¥1m ask: Li 2.5, Zhang 4. By assets Li is cheaper again. But this only matters when "if it closes, what can I sell for parts?" matters — for a brand‑and‑traffic business like a noodle stall, P/B is mostly a floor, not a useful comparison. P/B becomes critical for banks, heavy industry, real estate (assets are the business).

维度 4 — 比赚的钱前面再加上"租金、利息、税"。 老王 想了一下 — 直接比净利不公平: 老张 房租 8 万 / 年, 老李 房租 2 万 / 年 (店是 老李 自己的, 算"机会成本"才 2 万)。如果按"剔除房租 / 利息 / 税"后的 核心经营收益 比, 老张 是 18 + 8 = 26 万, 老李 是 25 + 2 = 27 万 — 几乎一样。"剔除融资结构和场地结构差异、只看经营本身"的这个指标, 公司估值里有对应物: EBITDA (息税折旧摊销前利润)。把企业总价值 (含债务) 除以 EBITDA, 就是 EV/EBITDA 倍数 — 它的好处是跨杠杆、跨折旧政策可比, 让两家"会计政策不同"的公司能放在同一个尺子上量。

Angle 4 — strip out rent, interest, tax. Comparing net profit penalizes Zhang's ¥80k rent vs Li's ¥20k (Li owns the storefront). Pre‑rent profit: Zhang ¥260k, Li ¥270k — basically tied. The corporate analogue is EBITDA; dividing total firm value (including debt) by EBITDA gives EV/EBITDA. Its virtue: leverage‑neutral, depreciation‑policy‑neutral — two firms with different financing and accounting choices sit on the same ruler.

维度 5 — 增长速度。 老王 最后一问: 这两家未来五年谁能更快增长? 老李 答: 我这家这两年新增午市, 客流稳定增加, 大概每年 8% 增长; 老张 说: 我这家所在位置最近写字楼搬走, 客流在下滑, 增长大概 0% 甚至 -2%。这下事情完全反转 — 即使 老张 P/E 略高, 但 老李 在增长上完胜。如果用一个把"P/E 和增长率"挂钩的指标, 比如 PEG = P/E ÷ 增长率%: 老李 PEG = 4/8 = 0.5; 老张 PEG = 5.6/0 = ∞ (无增长股的 PEG 没意义, 退回纯 P/E 看)。PEG 是 Peter Lynch 推广的简化指标: PEG ≤ 1 通常被认为相对便宜。当然这只是粗略的拇指法则, 不是真理。

Angle 5 — growth. Li added a lunch shift; foot traffic up ~8%/yr. Zhang's nearby office block moved out; growth 0 or −2%. Suddenly Li wins regardless of P/E. The metric that ties P/E to growth is PEG = P/E ÷ growth% (Peter Lynch): Li PEG = 4/8 = 0.5; Zhang PEG = 5.6/0 = ∞ (undefined for no‑growth — fall back to plain P/E). PEG ≤ 1 is a rough "cheap" rule‑of‑thumb, not gospel.

把五个维度并起来看: 在 P/S 上 老张 略便宜, 但在 P/E、P/B、增长和 PEG 上 老李 都领先, EV/EBITDA 上几乎打平。综合判断 — 应该买 老李。但 老王 注意到一件事: 每个倍数都讲了一个不同的故事。P/S 说"营收便宜", P/E 说"盈利便宜", P/B 说"资产便宜", EV/EBITDA 说"经营便宜", PEG 说"成长便宜"。没有一个倍数能单独决定哪家更好 — 必须几个倍数交叉看, 各自补足对方看不见的角度。

Putting all five together: Zhang is cheaper on P/S; Li wins on P/E, P/B, growth, PEG; EV/EBITDA is roughly tied. Buy Li. But notice: each multiple told a different story. P/S says "revenue is cheap", P/E says "earnings are cheap", P/B says "assets are cheap", EV/EBITDA says "operations are cheap", PEG says "growth is cheap". No single multiple decides anything alone — they cross‑check by covering the blind spots of one another.

老王 把面馆比较的五个维度记在本子上, 然后翻到下一页, 写: "茅台 P/E 30 倍, 五粮液 P/E 20 倍, 谁更值得买?"他突然意识到 — 这就是面馆问题的放大版。茅台和五粮液都是白酒龙头, 同样的逻辑可以用上: P/E、P/B、EV/EBITDA、P/S、PEG 五条线一起看, 谁在哪些倍数上贵了, 这些贵是因为增长更快还是只是市场偏好。Layer 2 会把这五个倍数写成正式公式, 把它们与 DCF 挂钩 (justified P/E、justified P/B), 解释为什么 P/B = ROE/r 这种关系成立。Layer 3 会拿 招行 vs 工行 (P/B 与 ROE 的关系) 和 茅台 vs 五粮液 (P/E premium 拆解) 两组真实 A 股案例打透。

Wang writes the five angles in his notebook, flips the page, and writes: "Moutai P/E 30, Wuliangye P/E 20 — which to buy?" He realizes this is the same problem scaled up. Two baijiu leaders, same five‑lens method: P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, P/S, PEG. Layer 2 formalises the five into equations and bridges them to DCF (justified P/E, justified P/B), explaining why $P/B \approx (\mathrm{ROE} - g)/(r - g)$. Layer 3 cracks it open with two real A‑share pairs: CMB vs ICBC (P/B–ROE link) and Moutai vs Wuliangye (decomposing the P/E premium).

Layer 2 · 定义与公式Definitions & Formulas

Layer 1 把五个倍数用 老李 和 老张 的面馆讲清楚了直觉。现在我们把每个倍数写成公式, 并把它们与 DCF (Ch.05) 对接 — 这样可以看清楚每个倍数背后隐含的 ROE、增长率、风险假设。

Layer 1 gave intuition through Old Li and Old Zhang. We now formalise each multiple and bridge it to DCF — exposing the ROE, growth, and risk assumptions each one implicitly carries.

五个核心倍数

The five core multiples

P/E — 市盈率 P/E — Price‑to‑Earnings
$$ \mathrm{P/E} = \frac{\text{Price}}{\text{EPS}} = \frac{\text{Market cap}}{\text{Net income}} $$(6.1)

"市场每元盈利愿付多少"。Trailing P/E 用 TTM 净利; forward P/E 用未来 12 月预测净利, 数值通常低于 trailing。

"How much the market pays per ¥1 of earnings." Trailing uses TTM; forward uses next‑12m forecast — usually lower than trailing.

P/B — 市净率 P/B — Price‑to‑Book
$$ \mathrm{P/B} = \frac{\text{Price}}{\text{BVPS}} = \frac{\text{Market cap}}{\text{Book equity}} $$(6.2)

资产密集行业 (银行、重工、地产) 的主指标。P/B < 1 意味着市场认为账面资产不可信 — 银行的常见情形。

Primary metric for asset‑heavy sectors (banks, heavy industry, real estate). P/B < 1 means the market doubts the book — common for banks.

EV/EBITDA — 企业价值倍数 EV/EBITDA — Enterprise Value multiple
$$ \mathrm{EV/EBITDA} = \frac{\text{Market cap} + \text{Debt} - \text{Cash}}{\text{EBITDA}} $$(6.3)

资本结构中性 (杠杆不影响), 折旧政策中性 (D&A 已加回)。跨杠杆 / 跨行业对比比 P/E 更稳健。

Leverage‑neutral and depreciation‑policy‑neutral. More robust cross‑leverage / cross‑sector than P/E.

P/S — 市销率 P/S — Price‑to‑Sales
$$ \mathrm{P/S} = \frac{\text{Market cap}}{\text{Revenue}} $$(6.4)

早期成长 (无利润、亏损) 公司的主指标。但忽略利润率差异 — 1 亿营收的高毛利软件公司和 1 亿营收的低毛利钢贸公司不能直接比。

For early‑growth (no profit) firms. But blind to margin — ¥100m revenue of high‑margin software vs ¥100m of low‑margin steel trading aren't comparable.

PEG — 增长调整后市盈率 PEG — growth‑adjusted P/E
$$ \mathrm{PEG} = \frac{\mathrm{P/E}}{g\% \text{ (annual earnings growth)}} $$(6.5)

Peter Lynch 在 One Up On Wall Street 推广。PEG ≤ 1 通常视为"价值-增长平衡"; PEG > 2 警惕高估。但 g 用 trailing 或 forward 差异大, 慎用。

Popularized by Peter Lynch in One Up On Wall Street. PEG ≤ 1 = "value‑growth balanced"; PEG > 2 = warning. Sensitive to whether $g$ is trailing or forward.

Damodaran, The Little Book of Valuation Ch. 4–5 · Brealey, Myers & Allen Ch. 4 · Lynch, One Up On Wall Street, 1989 · CFA Curriculum Level II

把倍数推回到 DCF — Justified Multiples

Bridging multiples to DCF — justified multiples

"P/E = 20"这个数本身只是一个比率, 不告诉你贵或便宜。但如果我们从 Gordon 模型 (Ch.05) 出发, 就能推出一个"给定 ROE、g、r, 应当对应多少 P/E"的公式 — 这就是 justified P/E。把市场 P/E 与 justified P/E 比, 才能判断便宜或贵。

"P/E = 20" alone tells you nothing about cheap or dear. Starting from Gordon, we can derive what P/E should equal given ROE, $g$, $r$ — the justified P/E. Comparing actual to justified judges cheap vs dear.

Gordon: $P_0 = D_1 / (r - g)$。设 $b$ 为留存比率 (1 − 派息率), 则 $D_1 = E_1 \cdot (1-b)$。除以 $E_1$ 得到 forward P/E:

Gordon: $P_0 = D_1 / (r - g)$. With retention $b = 1 -$ payout: $D_1 = E_1(1-b)$. Divide by $E_1$:

Justified Forward P/E Justified forward P/E
$$ \frac{P_0}{E_1} = \frac{1-b}{r-g} \quad\text{or}\quad \mathrm{P/E}_{\text{just}} = \frac{\text{payout}}{r-g} $$(6.6)

直觉: 高派息 ↑ P/E, 高 r ↓ P/E, 高 g ↑ P/E。但有约束: 增长率 g 与 ROE 通过留存挂钩 — $g = b \cdot \mathrm{ROE}$。所以增长率不是自由参数, 而是 ROE 和留存的乘积。这与 ROE 杜邦分解形成自洽 (M2 Ch.06)。

High payout ↑ P/E; high $r$ ↓ P/E; high $g$ ↑ P/E. Constraint: $g = b \cdot \mathrm{ROE}$ — growth not free; it's a product of retention and ROE. Self‑consistent with DuPont (M2 Ch.06).

Similarly for justified P/B. Substituting $E_1 = \mathrm{ROE} \cdot B_0$ into (6.6):

Justified P/B Justified P/B
$$ \mathrm{P/B}_{\text{just}} = \frac{\mathrm{ROE} - g}{r - g} $$(6.7)

关键直觉: P/B 主要由 ROE − r 驱动。当 ROE = r, P/B = 1; ROE > r, P/B > 1 (公司创造超额回报, 市场给溢价); ROE < r, P/B < 1 (公司毁灭价值, 市场打折)。这就是为什么银行 P/B < 1 — 市场认为银行的 ROE 不足以补偿其风险。

Key intuition: P/B is mostly driven by ROE − r. ROE = r → P/B = 1; ROE > r → P/B > 1 (firm creates excess returns, premium); ROE < r → P/B < 1 (value destruction, discount). Banks trade below P/B 1 because the market thinks their ROE doesn't compensate for their risk.

行业可比公司选择标准 (Damodaran)

Comparable selection criteria (Damodaran)

用倍数估值的前提是找到真正可比的公司。Damodaran 在 The Little Book of Valuation 列出 4 条筛选标准:

  1. 同行业: 业务模式相似 (不只是 SIC 代码相同 — 比如苏宁和京东都属"零售", 但模式完全不同)。
  2. 同增长阶段: 增长率、市占率成熟度接近。"成长股可比成长股, 价值股可比价值股"。
  3. 同盈利能力: ROE / ROIC 接近, 反映商业模式效率。
  4. 同风险: β, 杠杆, 经营波动率 (季度盈利标准差) 接近。

Damodaran's 4 screens for comparables: (1) same industry by business model, not SIC; (2) same growth stage; (3) similar ROE / ROIC (business‑model efficiency); (4) similar risk (β, leverage, earnings volatility).

反向用法 — 反推市场隐含假设

Reverse engineering — implied assumptions

把 (6.6) 反过来解 $g$: $g = r - (1-b)/\mathrm{P/E}$。若已知 r 和 payout, 一个市场 P/E 反映一个 g。例: P/E = 20, $r = 10\%$, payout = 60%: $g = 0.10 - 0.6/20 = 7\%$。市场隐含该公司永续增长 7%。

Solve (6.6) for $g$: $g = r - (1-b)/\mathrm{P/E}$. With P/E = 20, $r = 10\%$, payout 60%: $g = 10\% - 0.6/20 = 7\%$. The market implies 7% perpetual growth.

买入判断: 你自己估的 g (基于行业研究) 高于 7% → 当前估值偏低; 低于 7% → 偏高。这把"P/E = 20 是贵还是便宜"这个模糊问题, 翻译成"市场假设 g = 7%, 你的研究告诉你它能不能超过 7%"这个可证伪问题。反向 DCF / 反向倍数是 Damodaran 教学的核心方法。

Buy if your independent $g$ estimate > 7%; sell if < 7%. This turns "is P/E 20 cheap?" into "is the market's implicit 7% beatable?" — falsifiable. Reverse valuation is Damodaran's central pedagogical move.

周期股 P/E 反转之谜

The cyclical P/E inversion puzzle

周期股 (钢铁、煤炭、化工、地产) 有一个反直觉特征: 盈利最高时 P/E 最低, 盈利最低时 P/E 最高。原因: 盈利峰值时市场预期下行, 提前给低 P/E (分母大, 分子受预期压制); 盈利底部时市场预期反弹, 给高 P/E (分母小, 分子等待复苏)。结论: 对周期股, 低 P/E ≠ 便宜, 高 P/E ≠ 贵。应换用 P/B 或 PEG 与周期位置 (库存周期、产能利用率) 综合判断。Lynch 称之为 "the trick of cyclicals"。

Cyclical stocks (steel, coal, chemicals, real estate) show an inversion: P/E lowest at peak earnings, highest at trough. Because the market anticipates the next phase. Conclusion: for cyclicals, low P/E ≠ cheap, high P/E ≠ dear. Use P/B + capacity utilization or PEG with cycle context. Lynch calls it "the trick of cyclicals."

  1. Q1. 银行 P/E = 5, 半导体 P/E = 50。谁更便宜?Q1. Bank P/E 5, semicon P/E 50 — which cheaper?

    不可直接比较。P/E 在不同行业可比的前提是"假设增长率、ROE 与风险相似"。代入 (6.6):

    • 银行 P/E 5, $r = 10\%$, payout 30% → 隐含 $g = 10\% - 0.3/5 = 4\%$ — 但银行 ROE 仅 ~10%, 加 g 假设勉强合理。问题是市场 P/E 5 隐含的市场怀疑度很高 (相当于把 ROE 折扣到 7-8%)。
    • 半导体 P/E 50, $r = 12\%$ (高 β), payout 10% → 隐含 $g = 12\% - 0.1/50 \approx 11.8\%$ — 必须假设公司能持续 ~12% 增长。如果实际增长更高, 估值合理; 更低, 严重高估。

    正确比较: 每个行业相对其自身历史/同行。银行 P/E 5 比历史平均 7 低 → 在行业内偏便宜; 半导体 P/E 50 比历史平均 60 低 → 行业内也偏便宜。但跨行业比较应用 EV/EBITDA + ROIC (剔除税、杠杆、折旧政策差异), 或反推隐含 g 比较合理性。Damodaran: "永远不要直接跨行业比 P/E"。

    Not directly comparable. Implied $g$ at bank P/E 5 with $r=10\%$, payout 30% is 4% — okay given bank ROE ~10%. Semicon P/E 50 with $r=12\%$, payout 10% implies $g \approx 11.8\%$ — must believe ~12% growth holds. Compare each within sector vs history. Cross‑sector: EV/EBITDA + ROIC, or reverse implied $g$. Damodaran: "never directly compare P/E across sectors."

    Implied $g \approx 5.7\%$. Your 8% > 5.7% → undervalued → consider buying. Three safety checks: (1) confidence — own research or hearsay? does the moat support 8% perpetual? (2) consensus — if sell‑side also at 8%, no alpha; differentiated view only if consensus is lower; (3) falsification — can you identify why the market is wrong? If not, probably you are.   3. Q3. 一家钢铁公司 2021 年顶峰 P/E 仅 4 (净利创历史新高), 2023 年低谷 P/E 高达 30 (净利大幅下滑)。这是不是说 2021 年是"4 倍便宜买入良机", 2023 年是"30 倍贵了卖出"?Q3. A steel company: P/E 4 at 2021 peak earnings; P/E 30 at 2023 earnings trough. Was 2021 a "P/E 4 cheap buy" and 2023 a "P/E 30 dear sell"?

    • 2021 P/E 4 是危险信号: 钢铁价格在历史峰值, 利润率被周期顶部放大, 净利不可持续。市场已预知周期下行, 故拒绝给高 P/E — 用低 P/E "反向贴现"未来盈利下滑。买入 = 接刀子。
    • 2023 P/E 30 是机会信号: 净利被周期底部压低, 利润率低甚至亏损, P/E 分母 (E) 变小, 比率虚高。但市场已 pricing 周期反转, 当前股价可能已包含未来 1-2 年的复苏预期。卖出 = 在底部割肉。

    正确做法: 周期股不用 P/E, 用 P/B (账面价相对稳定, 不受周期扭曲) + 周期位置判断 (产能利用率、库存、价格 vs 历史中位)。或用 正常化 P/E (normalized P/E): 用整个周期平均盈利 (例如 10 年平均) 代替当前年度盈利。Shiller's CAPE (周期调整后市盈率) 在大盘指数层面是这个思想的应用。

    Opposite — the textbook "trick of cyclicals." 2021 P/E 4 is a warning: peak earnings won't last; market prices in the looming downturn with low P/E. 2023 P/E 30 is the opportunity: trough earnings collapse the denominator; market already prices recovery. Use P/B (insulated from cycle) + cycle indicators (capacity utilization, inventory, price vs historical median), or normalized P/E (Shiller CAPE‑style: 10y average earnings). Same essence as Old Wang's Mistake 1 (Moutai anchoring) — treating a snapshot number as eternal truth.

  4. Q4. 招行 ROE 16%, 工行 ROE 10.5%, 都用同一条 P/B 公式 (6.7) 算 justified 值, 但实际 P/B 都低于 justified。为什么"市场总是错"?或者是公式错?Q4. CMB ROE 16%, ICBC ROE 10.5%, both have actual P/B below the justified value from (6.7). Is "the market always wrong" or is the formula wrong?

    都不是 — 是公式假设与市场认知不一致。(6.7) 假设 ROE 永续不变, 但市场看到三件公式不知道的事:

    1. ROE 均值回归: 银行业的高 ROE 通常不可持续。监管 (资本充足率)、利率周期 (NIM 下行)、不良暴露 (信用周期) 都会把 ROE 拉回行业均值。市场提前给 P/B 折扣反映这一点。
    2. 账面资产的真实质量: 银行资产负债表上有大量"未爆雷"的隐藏不良。市场对账面价值的可信度打折 — 这相当于把 BVPS 的有效值降低。如果实际坏账率比披露高 1pp, BVPS 可能虚高 15-20%。
    3. 极端尾部风险: 银行有"流动性危机"的小概率大损失尾部 (Bear Stearns/Lehman 类型)。投资者要求额外风险溢价, 等价于把 $r_e$ 调高, 进而压低 justified P/B。

    对公式的修正: 把 (6.7) 改写为 $\mathrm{P/B} = (\mathrm{ROE}_{\text{sustainable}} - g)/(r_e + \pi - g)$, 其中 $\pi$ 是账面不确定性风险溢价。对银行 $\pi$ 通常加 2-3pp, 这就解释了 P/B 持续低于 1 的现象。Damodaran: 对金融机构使用 P/B 时必须考虑"账面质量"。

    结论: 不是市场错, 也不是公式错; 而是 (6.7) 是简化模型, 实际公式需要纳入"账面可信度"和"ROE 持续性"两个真实世界因素。这告诉你: 倍数估值的艺术在于辨识公式的简化在你这个 case 中是否成立。

    Neither — assumption mismatch. (6.7) assumes ROE is perpetual but the market sees: (1) ROE mean reversion (regulation, rate cycle, NPL cycle pull bank ROE to industry mean); (2) book quality (hidden NPL discounts BVPS — a 1 pp higher real NPL could inflate book by 15–20%); (3) extreme tail risk (liquidity crisis, Bear/Lehman type) demands extra premium, equivalent to lifting $r_e$. Modified: $\mathrm{P/B} = (\mathrm{ROE}_{\text{sustainable}} - g)/(r_e + \pi - g)$ with book‑quality premium $\pi$ (~2–3 pp for banks). Multiples valuation is an art of judging whether the formula's simplifications hold for the case at hand.

    从 27 降到 ~12 — 接近 60% 跌幅。茅台股价从 ~1,500 元跌到 ~650 元 (假设 EPS 保持稳定; 实际下跌中 EPS 也会同步走弱, 跌幅可能更大)。

    类比 2021 抱团股: 行为金融 Ch.04 讲过, 2021 末茅台 P/E 60 跌回 30 用的是同样机制 — 不是基本面立刻恶化, 而是市场重新评估 $g_2$ 假设。一旦市场对永续增长率从 7% 改 pricing 到 4%, 即使当前盈利不变, 估值立即重置。这就是为什么"P/E 高的股票更容易腰斩" — 它的 P/E 对 $g_2$ 假设的导数最大 (回顾 Ch.05 5.6 节)。

    反过来思考: 如果 $g_2$ 上调到 8% (例如海外市场打开), P/E justified 应到 $0.52 / (0.085 - 0.08) = 104$ — Gordon 奇点附近, 数学崩了。实际中 $g_2 = 8\%$ 远高于长期 GDP, 不应当作永续假设。这说明: 用 (6.6) 反推 $g_2$ 时, 必须用永续可持续的值, 不能用短期高速增长。Damodaran 反复强调: $g_2$ 在永续模型里数值很小, 但威力巨大。

    给 老王 的教训: 别只看"P/E 27 比五粮液高"或"P/E 27 比历史均值低", 要看 P/E 27 隐含的 $g_2$ 假设。如果你不认同这个 $g_2$, 你就不应该被 P/E 数字本身说服。Layer 1 老李 vs 老张 比较时 老王 算的是静态对比, 但真实市场是动态重定价过程 — 倍数随时可以变。这是相对估值最重要的告诫。

    Recompute justified P/E for Moutai at $r_e=8.5\%$, payout 52%, $g=4\%$: $0.52/(0.085-0.04) \approx 11.6$. From 27 → ~12, roughly a 60% drop. Stock from ~¥1,500 → ~¥650 (with EPS held constant; in practice EPS also weakens, so drop is worse). This is the 2021 herd reversion mechanism (Ch.04): not fundamentals collapsing, but the market re‑rating the $g_2$ assumption. High‑P/E stocks halve more easily because their derivative w.r.t. $g_2$ is largest (Ch.05 §5.6). Reversal: if $g_2 \to 8\%$, justified P/E hits 104, near the Gordon singularity — math breaks. So $g_2$ in a perpetuity must be small but is exceptionally powerful (Damodaran). Lesson for Old Wang: don't be persuaded by P/E 27 alone — examine the $g_2$ it implies.
